1. Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

Is section mein photon energy aur de-Broglie wavelength sabse zyada pucha jata hai.

‚Ä¢ Energy of a Photon: $E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
‚Ä¢ Shortcut: $E (\text{in eV}) \approx \frac{12400}{\lambda (\text{in \AA})}$ ya $\frac{1240}{\lambda (\text{in nm})}$
‚Ä¢ Einstein‚Äôs Photoelectric Equation: $K_{max} = eV_0 = h\nu - \phi$
(Yahan $V_0$ stopping potential hai aur $\phi$ (work function) = $h\nu_{threshold}$)
‚Ä¢ de-Broglie Wavelength: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mK}}$
‚Ä¢ For an electron: $\lambda \approx \sqrt{\frac{150}{V}} \text{ \AA} \approx \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}} \text{ \AA}$
Example: Agar ek metal ka work function $2.0 \text{ eV}$ hai aur uspar $4000 \text{ \AA}$ ki light girti hai, to stopping potential kya hoga?
Solution: $E = \frac{12400}{4000} = 3.1 \text{ eV}$.
Ab, $K_{max} = 3.1 - 2.0 = 1.1 \text{ eV}$. Isliye, $V_0 = 1.1 \text{ V}$.

2. Atomic Structure (Bohr‚Äôs Model)

*Formulas sirf hydrogen-like atoms ($Z = 1, 2, 3 \dots$) ke liye valid hain.

‚Ä¢ Radius of $n^{th}$ orbit: $r_n = 0.529 \frac{n^2}{Z} \text{ \AA}$
‚Ä¢ Velocity in $n^{th}$ orbit: $v_n = 2.19 \times 10^6 \frac{Z}{n} \text{ m/s}$
‚Ä¢ Energy in $n^{th}$ orbit: $E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2} \text{ eV}$
‚Ä¢ Wavelength: $\frac{1}{\lambda} = RZ^2 \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right)$
(Rydberg constant $R \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$)

3. Nuclear Physics

‚Ä¢ Mass-Energy Equivalence: $E = \Delta m \cdot c^2$
‚Ä¢ Unit: $1 \text{ amu} = 931.5 \text{ MeV}$
‚Ä¢ Nuclear Radius: $R = R_0 A^{1/3}$ ($R_0 \approx 1.2 \text{ fm}$)
‚Ä¢ Decay Law: $N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$
‚Ä¢ Half-life: $T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$
‚Ä¢ Activity (A): $A = \lambda N$
Example: Half-life $20 \text{ mins}$ hai, kitne time baad $75\%$ decay ho jayega?
Solution: $75\%$ decay = $25\%$ ($1/4$) bacha hai. $1 \xrightarrow{T_{1/2}} 1/2 \xrightarrow{T_{1/2}} 1/4$.
Do half-lives lagenge. Total time = $2 \times 20 = \mathbf{40 \text{ mins}}$.

4. Semiconductors

‚Ä¢ Mass Action Law: $n_e \cdot n_h = n_i^2$
‚Ä¢ Conductivity: $\sigma = e(n_e \mu_e + n_h \mu_h)$
‚Ä¢ Logic Gates: Practice AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR.

‚ö° 3. Electrostatics & Capacitance (High Weightage)

1. Coulomb's Law & Electric Field

‚Ä¢ Coulomb's Force: $F = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r^2}$ | $k = 9 \times 10^9 \text{ Nm}^2/\text{C}^2$
‚Ä¢ Electric Field (E): $E = \frac{F}{q} = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$
‚Ä¢ Infinite Wire: $E = \frac{2k\lambda}{r}$ | Infinite Sheet: $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0}$
‚Ä¢ Sphere (Outside): $E = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$ | Sphere (Inside): $E = 0$

2. Potential & Gauss‚Äôs Law

‚Ä¢ Potential (V): $V = \frac{kQ}{r}$ | Relation: $E = -\frac{dV}{dr}$
‚Ä¢ Potential Energy: $U = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r}$ | Work: $W = q\Delta V$
‚Ä¢ Gauss‚Äôs Law: $\phi = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{in}}{\epsilon_0}$

3. Electric Dipole

‚Ä¢ Dipole Moment: $\vec{p} = q(2l)$ (-ve to +ve)
‚Ä¢ Torque: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{p} \times \vec{E} = pE \sin\theta$
‚Ä¢ Energy: $U = -\vec{p} \cdot \vec{E} = -pE \cos\theta$
‚Ä¢ Axial Field: $E \approx \frac{2kp}{r^3}$ | Equatorial: $E \approx \frac{-kp}{r^3}$

4. Capacitance

‚Ä¢ Capacitance: $C = \frac{Q}{V}$ | Parallel Plate: $C = \frac{A\epsilon_0}{d}$
‚Ä¢ Dielectric (K): $C' = \frac{KA\epsilon_0}{d}$
‚Ä¢ Energy Stored: $U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$
‚Ä¢ Energy Density: $u = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_0 E^2$
Example (8 Drops Combine): Agar 8 small drops (charge q, radius r) combine hokar ek big drop banati hain, to potential kya hoga?

Solution: Charge $Q = 8q$. Volume $\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = 8 \times \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \implies R = 2r$.
Naya Potential $V' = \frac{k(8q)}{2r} = 4 \left( \frac{kq}{r} \right) = \mathbf{4V}$.

2. Magnetic Field (Standard Shapes)

‚Ä¢ Straight Wire (Infinite): $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$
‚Ä¢ Circular Loop (Center): $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$
‚Ä¢ Circular Loop (Axis): $B = \frac{\mu_0 I R^2}{2(R^2 + x^2)^{3/2}}$

üîå5. Electromagnetic Induction & AC

1. Electromagnetic Induction (EMI)

Magnetic field se electricity generate karne ka process.

‚Ä¢ Magnetic Flux ($\phi$): $\phi = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA \cos \theta$
‚Ä¢ Faraday's Law: $e = -\frac{d\phi}{dt}$ (EMF is rate of change of flux)
‚Ä¢ Lenz's Law: Induced current hamesha apne 'cause' ka oppose karta hai.
‚Ä¢ Motional EMF: $e = BvL$ (Wire moving in B-field)
‚Ä¢ Self-Inductance (L): $\phi = LI$ | Energy $U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$
‚Ä¢ Mutual Inductance (M): $\phi_2 = MI_1$

2. Alternating Current (AC)

‚Ä¢ RMS Value: $I_{rms} = \frac{I_0}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707 I_0$ (Gharon mein 220V RMS hota hai)
‚Ä¢ Reactance: $X_L = \omega L$ | $X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C}$
‚Ä¢ Impedance (Z): $Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2}$
‚Ä¢ Resonance ($X_L = X_C$): $f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}$ | Current maximum hota hai.
‚Ä¢ Power Factor: $\cos \phi = \frac{R}{Z}$ | $P_{avg} = V_{rms}I_{rms} \cos \phi$

3. Transformer

‚Ä¢ Relation: $\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p} = \frac{I_p}{I_s}$
‚Ä¢ Step-up: $N_s > N_p$ (Voltage badhata hai)
‚Ä¢ Step-down: $N_s < N_p$ (Voltage ghatata hai)

‚Ä¢ Snell's Law: $n_1 \sin i = n_2 \sin r$

‚Ä¢ Lens Formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$

üî•7. KTG & Thermodynamics (Thermal Physics)

1. Kinetic Theory of Gases (KTG)

Gas molecules ke microscopic behavior ka study.

‚Ä¢ Ideal Gas Eq: $PV = nRT = Nk_BT$
‚Ä¢ Pressure: $P = \frac{1}{3} \rho v_{rms}^2$
‚Ä¢ Velocities:
   - $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$ | $v_{avg} = \sqrt{\frac{8RT}{\pi M}}$ | $v_{mp} = \sqrt{\frac{2RT}{M}}$
‚Ä¢ Internal Energy (U): $U = \frac{f}{2}nRT$
‚Ä¢ Degrees of Freedom (f):
   - Monoatomic: $f=3$ | Diatomic: $f=5$ | Polyatomic: $f=6$

2. Laws of Thermodynamics

‚Ä¢ First Law (FLOT): $\Delta Q = \Delta U + W$ (Energy Conservation)
‚Ä¢ Sign Convention: Heat given (+ve), Work done by system (+ve)
‚Ä¢ $\gamma$ (Adiabatic Index): $\gamma = \frac{C_p}{C_v} = 1 + \frac{2}{f}$

3. Specific Heat & Heat Engines

‚Ä¢ Mayer's Relation: $C_p - C_v = R$
‚Ä¢ Carnot Efficiency ($\eta$): $\eta = 1 - \frac{T_{sink}}{T_{source}} = \frac{W}{Q_{in}}$
‚Ä¢ Refrigerator (COP $\beta$): $\beta = \frac{Q_{low}}{W} = \frac{T_{sink}}{T_{source} - T_{sink}}$
Note: Temperature hamesha Kelvin mein lein.

üìè9. ERROR ANALYSIS (FULL SUMMARY) ‚ñº

1. TYPES OF ERRORS (Galti Ke Prakaar)

  • üìâ Systematic Errors: Fixed pattern mein hoti hain (e.g., Instrumental error). Inhein calibration se door kiya ja sakta hai.
  • üé≤ Random Errors: Unpredictable hoti hain. Inhein kam karne ke liye zyada measurements lekar Mean nikalte hain.

2. MATHEMATICAL TERMS

Agar readings $a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n$ hain:

üîπ Mean Value ($a_m$): $\frac{\sum a_i}{n}$ (True Value)

üîπ Absolute Error ($\Delta a_i$): $|a_m - a_i|$ (Hamesha positive)

üîπ Mean Absolute Error ($\overline{\Delta a}$): $\frac{\sum |\Delta a_i|}{n}$

üîπ Percentage Error: $\frac{\overline{\Delta a}}{a_m} \times 100\%$

3. PROPAGATION OF ERRORS (Sabse Important)

Operation Formula Max Error
Addition/Subtraction$Z = A \pm B$$\Delta Z = \Delta A + \Delta B$
Product/Division$Z = AB$ or $A/B$$\frac{\Delta Z}{Z} = \frac{\Delta A}{A} + \frac{\Delta B}{B}$

üí° GOLDEN RULE: Errors hamesha ADD hote hain!

4. GENERAL ERROR FORMULA (JEE SPECIAL)

Agar $Z = \frac{A^p B^q}{C^r}$, toh maximum percentage error:

$\% \text{ Error in } Z = \left[ p\left(\frac{\Delta A}{A}\right) + q\left(\frac{\Delta B}{B}\right) + r\left(\frac{\Delta C}{C}\right) \right] \times 100$

Note: Denominator ki power ($r$) ko bhi positive hi lena hai.

5. ZERO ERROR IN INSTRUMENTS

‚úÖ Positive Zero Error: Final reading se Subtract karo.

‚úÖ Negative Zero Error: Final reading mein Add karo.

2. Sensitivity: Jis variable ki power sabse zyada hai, wo total error mein sabse zyada contribute karta hai.

3. Log Method: Complex formulas ke liye dono side $\ln$ lekar differentiate kar do.

‚öôÔ∏è10. WORK, ENERGY & POWER (FULL CHAPTER) ‚ñº

1. WORK DONE (Karya)

Work by Constant Force: $W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{S} = FS \cos \theta$

Work by Variable Force: $W = \int \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{x}$ (Area under F-x graph).

Conservative vs Non-Conservative Forces:

  • Conservative: Path par depend nahi karta (Gravity, Electrostatic). Closed loop work = 0.
  • Non-Conservative: Path par depend karta hai (Friction, Viscosity).

2. KINETIC ENERGY & WORK-ENERGY THEOREM

Kinetic Energy (K.E.): $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{p^2}{2m}$ (p = momentum)

üöÄ Work-Energy Theorem (The Master Key):

$W_{total} = \Delta K = K_f - K_i$

(Sum of work by ALL forces = Change in Kinetic Energy)

3. POTENTIAL ENERGY (P.E.)

Define sirf Conservative Forces ke liye hoti hai: $F = -\frac{dU}{dx}$

  • üìç Gravitational P.E.: $U = mgh$
  • üìç Elastic Spring P.E.: $U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$
Equilibrium Condition U Value
Stable$d^2U/dx^2 > 0$Minimum
Unstable$d^2U/dx^2 < 0$Maximum

4. POWER & EFFICIENCY

Instantaneous Power: $P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} = Fv \cos \theta$

Efficiency ($\eta$): $\frac{P_{output}}{P_{input}} \times 100\%$

5. COLLISIONS (Takkar)

Momentum Conservation: Har type ke collision mein linear momentum conserve rehta hai.

Coefficient of Restitution (e): $e = \frac{\text{Velocity of Separation}}{\text{Velocity of Approach}}$

  • üîπ Elastic (e=1): No K.E. loss.
  • üîπ Inelastic (0 < e < 1): K.E. loss hota hai.
  • üîπ Perfectly Inelastic (e=0): Bodies stick together.
