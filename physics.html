1. Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

Is section mein photon energy aur de-Broglie wavelength sabse zyada pucha jata hai.

‚Ä¢ Energy of a Photon: $E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
‚Ä¢ Shortcut: $E (\text{in eV}) \approx \frac{12400}{\lambda (\text{in \AA})}$ ya $\frac{1240}{\lambda (\text{in nm})}$
‚Ä¢ Einstein‚Äôs Photoelectric Equation: $K_{max} = eV_0 = h\nu - \phi$
(Yahan $V_0$ stopping potential hai aur $\phi$ (work function) = $h\nu_{threshold}$)
‚Ä¢ de-Broglie Wavelength: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mK}}$
‚Ä¢ For an electron: $\lambda \approx \sqrt{\frac{150}{V}} \text{ \AA} \approx \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}} \text{ \AA}$
Example: Agar ek metal ka work function $2.0 \text{ eV}$ hai aur uspar $4000 \text{ \AA}$ ki light girti hai, to stopping potential kya hoga?
Solution: $E = \frac{12400}{4000} = 3.1 \text{ eV}$.
Ab, $K_{max} = 3.1 - 2.0 = 1.1 \text{ eV}$. Isliye, $V_0 = 1.1 \text{ V}$.

2. Atomic Structure (Bohr‚Äôs Model)

*Formulas sirf hydrogen-like atoms ($Z = 1, 2, 3 \dots$) ke liye valid hain.

‚Ä¢ Radius of $n^{th}$ orbit: $r_n = 0.529 \frac{n^2}{Z} \text{ \AA}$
‚Ä¢ Velocity in $n^{th}$ orbit: $v_n = 2.19 \times 10^6 \frac{Z}{n} \text{ m/s}$
‚Ä¢ Energy in $n^{th}$ orbit: $E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2} \text{ eV}$
‚Ä¢ Wavelength: $\frac{1}{\lambda} = RZ^2 \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right)$
(Rydberg constant $R \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$)

3. Nuclear Physics

‚Ä¢ Mass-Energy Equivalence: $E = \Delta m \cdot c^2$
‚Ä¢ Unit: $1 \text{ amu} = 931.5 \text{ MeV}$
‚Ä¢ Nuclear Radius: $R = R_0 A^{1/3}$ ($R_0 \approx 1.2 \text{ fm}$)
‚Ä¢ Decay Law: $N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$
‚Ä¢ Half-life: $T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$
‚Ä¢ Activity (A): $A = \lambda N$
Example: Half-life $20 \text{ mins}$ hai, kitne time baad $75\%$ decay ho jayega?
Solution: $75\%$ decay = $25\%$ ($1/4$) bacha hai. $1 \xrightarrow{T_{1/2}} 1/2 \xrightarrow{T_{1/2}} 1/4$.
Do half-lives lagenge. Total time = $2 \times 20 = \mathbf{40 \text{ mins}}$.
