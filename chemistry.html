1. ELECTRONIC EFFECTS (Sabse Important)

Ye effects decide karte hain ki molecule kitna reactive ya stable hoga.

  • üü¢ Inductive Effect (I): $\sigma$-electrons ka displacement.
      (+I: Alkyl groups | -I: $NO_2, CN, F, COOH$)
  • üü¢ Resonance (M): $\pi$-electrons ka delocalization.
      (+M: $OH, NH_2, OR$ | -M: $C=O, NO_2, CN$)
  • üü¢ Hyperconjugation: $\sigma$-bond (C-H) aur $\pi$-orbital ka overlap. Ise "Baker-Nathan effect" bhi kehte hain.

2. STABILITY OF REACTION INTERMEDIATES

üîπ Carbocation ($C^+$): Resonance > Hyperconjugation > Inductive.

üîπ Carbanion ($C^-$): Stable by -I and -M groups.

üîπ Free Radicals: Follows Carbocation stability pattern.

3. ACIDIC & BASIC STRENGTH

Acidic Strength: Directly proportional to -M and -I groups. (Stable anion = Strong Acid).

Basic Strength: Directly proportional to +M and +I groups. (Lone pair availability).

4. ISOMERISM (Short Glance)

‚Ä¢ Structural: Chain, Position, Functional, Metamerism, Tautomerism.

‚Ä¢ Stereoisomerism: Geometrical (Cis-Trans, E-Z) and Optical (Chiral center, Enantiomers, Meso).

5. REAGENTS & PURIFICATION

Electrophiles ($E^+$): Electron loving ($AlCl_3, BF_3, NO_2^+$).

Nucleophiles ($Nu^-$): Nucleus loving ($OH^-, CN^-, NH_3$).

Analysis: Lassaigne's Test (N, S, Halogens detection) | Kjeldahl‚Äôs Method (N estimation).

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. Aromaticity: Cyclic, Planar, Fully Conjugated + $(4n+2)\pi$ electrons (Huckel's Rule).

2. Stability Order (A-H-I): Hamesha pehle Aromaticity check karein, fir Resonance (M), fir Hyperconjugation, fir Inductive Effect.

3. Tautomerism: $\alpha$-hydrogen ka hona mandatory hai.

1. KOSSEL-LEWIS APPROACH & OCTET RULE

Atoms apne valence shell mein 8 electrons poore karne ke liye bond banate hain.

  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Exceptions:
  • ‚Ä¢ Incomplete Octet: $LiCl, BeH_2, BCl_3$

  • ‚Ä¢ Odd Electron: $NO, NO_2$

2. IONIC BOND & FAJAN‚ÄôS RULE

Lattice Enthalpy: Energy required to break 1 mole ionic compound into gaseous ions.

üöÄ Fajan's Rule: Covalent character $\uparrow$ jab:

Cation small ho, Anion bada ho, ya Cation par charge zyada ho.

3. VSEPR THEORY (Shape and Geometry)

Repulsion Order: LP-LP > LP-BP > BP-BP

  • 2 BP: Linear
  • 3 BP: Trigonal Planar | 2 BP + 1 LP: Bent
  • 4 BP: Tetrahedral | 3 BP + 1 LP: Pyramidal
  • 5 BP: TBP | 4 BP + 1 LP: See-saw

4. HYBRIDIZATION (The Game Changer)

Formula: $H = \frac{1}{2} [V + M - C + A]$

5. MOLECULAR ORBITAL THEORY (MOT)

Bond Order (B.O.): $\frac{1}{2} [N_b - N_a]$

‚úÖ Stability $\propto$ B.O. $\propto \frac{1}{\text{Bond Length}}$

‚úÖ Unpaired electrons = Paramagnetic | All paired = Diamagnetic

6. DIPOLE MOMENT & H-BONDING

Dipole Moment ($\mu$): $q \times d$. (If $\mu=0 \to$ Non-polar).

H-Bonding: Jab H atoms F, O, ya N ke beech ho.

  • Intermolecular: Between different molecules ($H_2O, NH_3$).
  • Intramolecular: Within the same molecule (o-nitrophenol).

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. B.O. Shortcut: 14 electrons ko 3.0 maano. Har $\pm 1$ electron par B.O. 0.5 kam karo.

2. Lone Pair: $LP = H - (\text{Number of Surrounding Atoms})$.

3. Isoelectronic: Species jinke electrons same hain, unka Bond Order hamesha same hota hai (e.g., $N_2, CO$).
