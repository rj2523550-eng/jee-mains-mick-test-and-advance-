1. ELECTRONIC EFFECTS (Sabse Important)

Ye effects decide karte hain ki molecule kitna reactive ya stable hoga.

  • üü¢ Inductive Effect (I): $\sigma$-electrons ka displacement.
      (+I: Alkyl groups | -I: $NO_2, CN, F, COOH$)
  • üü¢ Resonance (M): $\pi$-electrons ka delocalization.
      (+M: $OH, NH_2, OR$ | -M: $C=O, NO_2, CN$)
  • üü¢ Hyperconjugation: $\sigma$-bond (C-H) aur $\pi$-orbital ka overlap. Ise "Baker-Nathan effect" bhi kehte hain.

2. STABILITY OF REACTION INTERMEDIATES

üîπ Carbocation ($C^+$): Resonance > Hyperconjugation > Inductive.

üîπ Carbanion ($C^-$): Stable by -I and -M groups.

üîπ Free Radicals: Follows Carbocation stability pattern.

3. ACIDIC & BASIC STRENGTH

Acidic Strength: Directly proportional to -M and -I groups. (Stable anion = Strong Acid).

Basic Strength: Directly proportional to +M and +I groups. (Lone pair availability).

4. ISOMERISM (Short Glance)

‚Ä¢ Structural: Chain, Position, Functional, Metamerism, Tautomerism.

‚Ä¢ Stereoisomerism: Geometrical (Cis-Trans, E-Z) and Optical (Chiral center, Enantiomers, Meso).

5. REAGENTS & PURIFICATION

Electrophiles ($E^+$): Electron loving ($AlCl_3, BF_3, NO_2^+$).

Nucleophiles ($Nu^-$): Nucleus loving ($OH^-, CN^-, NH_3$).

Analysis: Lassaigne's Test (N, S, Halogens detection) | Kjeldahl‚Äôs Method (N estimation).

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. Aromaticity: Cyclic, Planar, Fully Conjugated + $(4n+2)\pi$ electrons (Huckel's Rule).

2. Stability Order (A-H-I): Hamesha pehle Aromaticity check karein, fir Resonance (M), fir Hyperconjugation, fir Inductive Effect.

3. Tautomerism: $\alpha$-hydrogen ka hona mandatory hai.

üíé CHEMICAL BONDING (SHORT NOTES) ‚ñº

1. KOSSEL-LEWIS APPROACH & OCTET RULE

Atoms apne valence shell mein 8 electrons poore karne ke liye bond banate hain.

  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Exceptions:
  • ‚Ä¢ Incomplete Octet: $LiCl, BeH_2, BCl_3$
  • ‚Ä¢ Expanded Octet: $PCl_5, SF_6, IF_7$ (D-orbitals ki wajah se)
  • ‚Ä¢ Odd Electron: $NO, NO_2$

2. IONIC BOND & FAJAN‚ÄôS RULE

Lattice Enthalpy: Energy required to break 1 mole ionic compound into gaseous ions.

üöÄ Fajan's Rule: Covalent character $\uparrow$ jab:

Cation small ho, Anion bada ho, ya Cation par charge zyada ho.

3. VSEPR THEORY (Shape and Geometry)

Repulsion Order: LP-LP > LP-BP > BP-BP

  • 2 BP: Linear
  • 3 BP: Trigonal Planar | 2 BP + 1 LP: Bent
  • 4 BP: Tetrahedral | 3 BP + 1 LP: Pyramidal
  • 5 BP: TBP | 4 BP + 1 LP: See-saw

4. HYBRIDIZATION (The Game Changer)

Formula: $H = \frac{1}{2} [V + M - C + A]$

H Value Hybridization Geometry
2spLinear
3sp¬≤Trigonal Planar
4sp¬≥Tetrahedral
5sp¬≥dTBP

5. MOLECULAR ORBITAL THEORY (MOT)

Bond Order (B.O.): $\frac{1}{2} [N_b - N_a]$

‚úÖ Stability $\propto$ B.O. $\propto \frac{1}{\text{Bond Length}}$

‚úÖ Unpaired electrons = Paramagnetic | All paired = Diamagnetic

6. DIPOLE MOMENT & H-BONDING

Dipole Moment ($\mu$): $q \times d$. (If $\mu=0 \to$ Non-polar).

H-Bonding: Jab H atoms F, O, ya N ke beech ho.

  • Intermolecular: Between different molecules ($H_2O, NH_3$).
  • Intramolecular: Within the same molecule (o-nitrophenol).

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. B.O. Shortcut: 14 electrons ko 3.0 maano. Har $\pm 1$ electron par B.O. 0.5 kam karo.

2. Lone Pair: $LP = H - (\text{Number of Surrounding Atoms})$.

3. Isoelectronic: Species jinke electrons same hain, unka Bond Order hamesha same hota hai (e.g., $N_2, CO$).

üß± COORDINATION COMPOUNDS (MASTER SHEET) ‚ñº

1. WERNER‚ÄôS THEORY

  • üî¥ Primary Valency: Ionizable (Oxidation state ke barabar).
  • üî¥ Secondary Valency: Non-ionizable (Coordination number). Geometry decide karti hai.

2. NOMENCLATURE & LIGANDS

Chelating Ligands: Jo ring banate hain aur stability badhate hain (e.g., $en, ox^{2-}$).

Ambidentate: Do alag atoms se donate karne wale (e.g., $SCN^-$ and $NCS^-$).

IUPAC Rule: Ligands (Alphabetical) + Metal + (Oxidation State in Roman).

3. ISOMERISM (Most Important)

üîπ Structural: Ionization, Linkage, Hydrate, Coordination.

üîπ Geometrical (GI): Cis-Trans (Square planar $MA_2B_2$ and Octahedral).

üîπ Optical (OI): Non-superimposable mirror images. (Chelating ligands wale octahedral hamesha OI dikhate hain).

4. VALENCE BOND THEORY (VBT)

  • ‚úîÔ∏è $sp^3$: Tetrahedral | $dsp^2$: Square Planar
  • ‚úîÔ∏è $d^2sp^3$: Inner orbital | $sp^3d^2$: Outer orbital
  • ‚úîÔ∏è Magnetic Moment ($\mu$): $\sqrt{n(n+2)}$ BM ($n$ = unpaired electrons)

5. CRYSTAL FIELD THEORY (CFT)

Octahedral Splitting: $t_{2g}$ (niche) aur $e_g$ (upar).

Spectrochemical Series: Ligand strength ka order ($CO > CN^- > en > NH_3 > \dots > F^- > Cl^-$).

‚ö†Ô∏è Strong Field Ligands (SFL): Pairing karwa dete hain (Low Spin).

6. SYNERGIC BONDING IN CARBONYLS

Metal apne electrons CO ke anti-bonding orbital mein back-donate karta hai. Isse M-C bond strong aur C-O bond weak ho jata hai.

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. SFL Pairing: $CN^-, CO, en, NH_3$ dikhe toh aankh band karke pairing karwa do (Generally).

2. EAN Rule: $EAN = Z - (O.S.) + 2 \times (C.N.)$. Agar Noble gas number aaye toh stable hai.

3. Optical Inactivity: Agar molecule mein Plane of Symmetry (POS) hai, toh wo OI nahi dikhayega.

üî• THERMODYNAMICS & ENERGETICS ‚ñº

1. BASIC TERMS (Terminology)

System: Wo part jise hum study kar rahe hain.

  • Open: Matter + Energy exchange hoti hai.
  • Closed: Sirf Energy exchange hoti hai.
  • Isolated: Kuch bhi exchange nahi hota (Ideal Case).

üîπ State Functions: Initial aur Final state par depend karte hain ($\Delta U, \Delta H, \Delta G, \Delta S$).
üîπ Path Functions: Raste (path) par depend karte hain ($w, q$).

2. FIRST LAW (FLOT)

$\Delta U = q + w$

Chemistry mein Work: $w = -P_{ext} \Delta V$

‚úÖ Expansion: $w$ is negative (-ve).
‚úÖ Compression: $w$ is positive (+ve).

3. ENTHALPY ($\Delta H$)

Constant pressure par heat exchange: $\Delta H = \Delta U + \Delta n_g RT$

üìå $\Delta n_g$: (Total moles of gaseous products) - (Total moles of gaseous reactants).

4. THERMOCHEMISTRY

Hess's Law: Total enthalpy change same rehta hai chahe reaction kitne bhi steps mein ho.

Bond Enthalpy: $\Delta H_{reaction} = \sum (\text{B.E. Reactants}) - \sum (\text{B.E. Products})$

5. SECOND LAW & ENTROPY (S)

Universe ki randomness (Entropy) badh rahi hai: $\Delta S = \frac{q_{rev}}{T}$

Spontaneity: Agar $\Delta S_{total} > 0$, toh process spontaneous hoga.

6. GIBBS FREE ENERGY ($\Delta G$)

$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$

  • üöÄ $\Delta G < 0$: Spontaneous (Apne aap hoga).
  • üöÄ $\Delta G > 0$: Non-spontaneous.
  • üöÄ $\Delta G = 0$: Equilibrium.

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. $\Delta n_g$ Calculation: Sirf "Gas" phase wale moles ginn-na hai. Solid/Liquid ko galti se bhi mat lena.

2. Physics vs Chemistry: Chemistry mein system par work ho toh Positive, system kare toh Negative.

3. Spontaneity Master Rule: Agar $\Delta H = -ve$ aur $\Delta S = +ve$ hai, toh wo reaction hamesha spontaneous hogi (at all T).

üß™ P-BLOCK ELEMENTS (GROUP 13-18) ‚ñº

1. BORON & CARBON FAMILY (GP 13-14)

üîπ Inert Pair Effect: Group mein niche jaane par lower oxidation state (+1) zyada stable hoti hai.

üîπ Diborane ($B_2H_6$): Isme Banana Bond (3c-2e bond) hota hai.

üîπ Silicates & Zeolites: Basic unit $SiO_4^{4-}$. Zeolites (like ZSM-5) alcohol ko petrol mein badalte hain.

2. NITROGEN & OXYGEN FAMILY (GP 15-16)

üîπ Nitric Acid ($HNO_3$): Metals ke saath reactions (Conc. vs Dilute) hamesha puchi jati hain.

üîπ Phosphorus Oxoacids: $H_3PO_2$ (monobasic), $H_3PO_3$ (dibasic), $H_3PO_4$ (tribasic).

üîπ Sulphur Oxoacids: Marshall‚Äôs acid ($H_2S_2O_8$) mein peroxide bond hota hai.

3. HALOGENS & NOBLE GASES (GP 17-18)

üîπ Electron Gain Enthalpy: Order $\to Cl > F > Br > I$. (Cl ki sabse zyada hai).

üîπ Interhalogens: Inki shapes ($ClF_3$ - T-shape, $IF_7$ - PBP) VSEPR se aati hain.

üîπ Xenon Compounds: $XeF_4$ (Square Planar), $XeF_2$ (Linear), $XeO_3$ (Pyramidal).

üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

1. Bond Angle Trend: Group mein niche jaane par hydride bond angle ghatt-ta hai ($NH_3 > PH_3 > AsH_3$).

2. Back Bonding: $BF_3$ mein $p\pi-p\pi$ bonding ki wajah se ye $BI_3$ se weak Lewis acid hai.

3. Oxidizing Power: $F_2$ puri periodic table ka sabse strong oxidizing agent hai.

‚öñÔ∏è EQUILIBRIUM (CHEMICAL & IONIC) ‚ñº

PART A: CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM

1. Law of Mass Action: Equilibrium par, Rate of Forward reaction = Rate of Backward reaction.

2. Equilibrium Constants: $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n_g}$
(Note: K sirf Temperature par depend karta hai)

3. Reaction Quotient (Q):

  • üî∏ $Q < K$: Reaction Forward jayegi.
  • üî∏ $Q > K$: Reaction Backward jayegi.
  • üî∏ $Q = K$: Equilibrium state.

4. Le-Chatelier‚Äôs Principle:

  • Concentration badhao $\to$ Opposite side shift.
  • Pressure badhao $\to$ Shift towards less gaseous moles.
  • Temp badhao $\to$ Endothermic reaction forward jayegi.

PART B: IONIC EQUILIBRIUM

1. Ostwald‚Äôs Dilution Law: Weak electrolytes ke liye, $\alpha = \sqrt{\frac{K_a}{C}}$.

2. pH Calculation:

  • Weak Acid: $pH = \frac{1}{2}(pK_a - \log C)$
  • WA + SB Salt: $pH = 7 + \frac{1}{2}(pK_a + \log C)$
  • SA + WB Salt: $pH = 7 - \frac{1}{2}(pK_b + \log C)$

    • 3. Buffer Solutions & Ksp:

    üîπ Henderson Equation: $pH = pK_a + \log \frac{[Salt]}{[Acid]}$

    üîπ Solubility Product ($K_{sp}$): Precipitation tab hoga jab $Q_{sp} > K_{sp}$.

    üîπ Common Ion Effect: Common ion ki presence mein solubility ghat jati hai.

    üëë JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

    1. Solid/Liquid Ignore Karo: $K_c$ ya $K_p$ likhte waqt Pure Solids aur Liquids ko 1 maante hain.

    2. Van't Hoff Equation: $\log \frac{K_2}{K_1} = \frac{\Delta H}{2.303R} \left( \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2} \right)$.

    3. Buffer Pehchano: Agar Weak Acid + Salt (with SB) dikhe, toh seedha Henderson lagao.

    1. BOHR‚ÄôS ATOMIC MODEL (Sabse Important)

    Valid sirf single electron species ke liye ($H, He^+, Li^{2+}$).

  • üîπ Radius: $r_n = 0.529 \frac{n^2}{Z} \text{ \AA}$
  • üîπ Velocity: $v_n = 2.18 \times 10^6 \frac{Z}{n} \text{ m/s}$
  • üîπ Energy: $E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2} \text{ eV/atom}$

    • Note: T.E. = -(K.E.) = 1/2 P.E.

    2. HYDROGEN SPECTRUM

    3. QUANTUM MECHANICAL MODEL

    • üìç Principal (n): Shell/Size (1, 2, 3...)
    • üìç Azimuthal (l): Subshell/Shape (0 to n-1).
          l=0 (s), 1 (p), 2 (d), 3 (f)

    4. CONFIGURATION RULES

    ‚úÖ Pauli: Max 2 electrons per orbital with opposite spin.

    ‚úÖ Aufbau: Electrons fill lower energy orbitals first (n+l rule).

    ‚úÖ Hund: Pairing tab tak nahi hogi jab tak har orbital half-filled na ho jaye.

    üî• JEE MAINS GOLDEN TRICKS

    2. Magnetic Moment ($\mu$): $\sqrt{n(n+2)} \text{ BM}$ ($n$ = unpaired electrons).

    3. $\lambda$ Shortcut: Longest $\lambda \to$ Min energy gap. Shortest $\lambda \to$ Max energy gap.
