üìê1. Coordinate Geometry (Straight Lines & Conics)

1. Straight Lines (Saral Rekha)

‚Ä¢ Distance: $d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$
‚Ä¢ Slope (m): $m = \tan \theta = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$
‚Ä¢ Angle between Lines: $\tan \theta = |\frac{m_1 - m_2}{1 + m_1m_2}|$
‚Ä¢ Parallel: $m_1 = m_2$ | Perpendicular: $m_1m_2 = -1$
‚Ä¢ Point to Line Distance: $d = \frac{|ax_1+by_1+c|}{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}$

2. Circles (Vritt)

‚Ä¢ Standard Eqn: $x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0$
‚Ä¢ Center: $(-g, -f)$ | Radius: $\sqrt{g^2+f^2-c}$
‚Ä¢ Condition of Tangency: $c^2 = r^2(1+m^2)$
‚Ä¢ Length of Tangent: $L = \sqrt{S_1}$ | Orthogonal: $2g_1g_2 + 2f_1f_2 = c_1 + c_2$

3. Conic Sections (Quick Summary)

Feature Parabola ($y^2=4ax$) Ellipse Hyperbola
Eccentricity (e) $e = 1$ $\sqrt{1 - \frac{b^2}{a^2}}$ $\sqrt{1 + \frac{b^2}{a^2}}$
Focus $(a, 0)$ $(\pm ae, 0)$ $(\pm ae, 0)$
Latus Rectum $4a$ $2b^2/a$ $2b^2/a$

4. Golden Tricks for JEE

‚Ä¢ T=0 Rule: Tangent ke liye $x^2 \to xx_1, x \to \frac{x+x_1}{2}$ replace karein.
‚Ä¢ Director Circle: Locus of $\perp$ tangents.
  - Circle: $x^2+y^2=2r^2$ | Ellipse: $x^2+y^2=a^2+b^2$

üìò Calculus (Limits, Continuity & Differentiation)

1. Limits (Seema)

Indeterminate Forms:

$\frac{0}{0}, \frac{\infty}{\infty}, \infty - \infty, 0 \times \infty, 1^\infty, 0^0, \infty^0$

‚Ä¢ L'Hopital's Rule: If form is $\frac{0}{0}$ or $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$, then $\lim \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$

‚Ä¢ Standard Limits:
$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$ | $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1$ | $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1}{x} = 1$
$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\ln(1+x)}{x} = 1$ | $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2}$

üöÄ $1^\infty$ Shortcut: If $\lim_{x \to a} [f(x)]^{g(x)}$ is $1^\infty$, then Result = $e^{\lim_{x \to a} g(x)[f(x)-1]}$

2. Continuity & Differentiability

‚Ä¢ Continuity: $LHL = RHL = f(a)$
‚Ä¢ Differentiability: Graph mein "Sharp Corner" ya "Break" nahi hona chahiye.
‚Ä¢ Note: Every differentiable function is continuous, but vice-versa is NOT true (e.g., $|x|$ at $x=0$).

3. Differentiation Formulas

Function Derivative $\frac{d}{dx}$
$x^n$$nx^{n-1}$
$\sin x$$\cos x$
$\cos x$$-\sin x$
$\tan x$$\sec^2 x$
$e^x$$e^x$
$a^x$$a^x \ln a$
$\ln x$$1/x$
$\tan^{-1} x$$\frac{1}{1+x^2}$

4. Rules & Applications

‚Ä¢ Product Rule: $(uv)' = u'v + uv'$
‚Ä¢ Quotient Rule: $(\frac{u}{v})' = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$
‚Ä¢ Tangent Slope: $m = \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)_{(x_1, y_1)}$
‚Ä¢ Normal Slope: $m_n = -\frac{1}{dy/dx}$

üåü GOLDEN TRICKS FOR JEE

‚Ä¢ Expansion Series: $\sin x \approx x - \frac{x^3}{6}$, $e^x \approx 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2}$. (L'Hopital se fast hai!)
‚Ä¢ Sandwich Theorem: If $f(x) \le g(x) \le h(x)$ and $\lim f = \lim h = L$, then $\lim g = L$.

üìô Calculus Part 2 (Integration & Differential Eqns)

1. Indefinite Integration

‚Ä¢ Basic: $\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$ | $\int \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln|x| + C$

‚Ä¢ Trig: $\int \sin x dx = -\cos x + C$ | $\int \sec^2 x dx = \tan x + C$

‚Ä¢ Exp: $\int e^x dx = e^x + C$ | $\int a^x dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln a} + C$

‚≠ê ILATE Rule (Parts): $\int uv \, dx = u \int v \, dx - \int (u' \int v \, dx) dx$

2. Special Integrals (Mains Favorite)

‚Ä¢ $\int \frac{dx}{x^2+a^2} = \frac{1}{a}\tan^{-1}(\frac{x}{a}) + C$
‚Ä¢ $\int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{a^2-x^2}} = \sin^{-1}(\frac{x}{a}) + C$
‚Ä¢ $\int \sqrt{a^2-x^2} dx = \frac{x}{2}\sqrt{a^2-x^2} + \frac{a^2}{2}\sin^{-1}(\frac{x}{a}) + C$

3. Definite Integration & AUC

‚Ä¢ King's Property: $\int_a^b f(x) dx = \int_a^b f(a+b-x) dx$

‚Ä¢ Even/Odd: If $f(-x)=-f(x)$, $\int_{-a}^a f(x) dx = 0$

‚Ä¢ AUC: Area between curves = $\int_a^b |f(x) - g(x)| dx$

4. Differential Equations (D.E.)

‚Ä¢ Linear D.E. Form: $\frac{dy}{dx} + Py = Q$

‚Ä¢ Integrating Factor (I.F.): $e^{\int P dx}$

‚Ä¢ Solution: $y(I.F.) = \int Q(I.F.) dx + C$

üåü GOLDEN TRICKS

‚Ä¢ Wallis Formula: $\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^n x \, dx$ solve karne ke liye iska use karein.
‚Ä¢ Periodic: $\int_0^{nT} f(x) dx = n \int_0^T f(x) dx$ (where T is period).

üìå Vector Algebra (Short Notes)

1. Basic Concepts

‚Ä¢ Magnitude: Agar $\vec{a} = x\hat{i} + y\hat{j} + z\hat{k}$, toh $|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$
‚Ä¢ Unit Vector: $\hat{a} = \frac{\vec{a}}{|\vec{a}|}$ (Iska magnitude 1 hota hai)
‚Ä¢ Direction Cosines: $l^2 + m^2 + n^2 = 1$

2. Dot Product (Scalar Product)

‚Ä¢ Formula: $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}| \cos\theta$
‚Ä¢ Perpendicularity: Agar $\vec{a} \perp \vec{b} \implies \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0$
‚Ä¢ Projection: Projection of $\vec{a}$ on $\vec{b} = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|}$

3. Cross Product (Vector Product)

‚Ä¢ Formula: $\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}| \sin\theta \, \hat{n}$
‚Ä¢ Determinant Form:
$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{vmatrix}$
‚Ä¢ Area of Triangle: $\frac{1}{2} |\vec{a} \times \vec{b}|$

4. Triple Products (High Yield)

‚Ä¢ Scalar Triple Product (STP): $[\vec{a} \vec{b} \vec{c}] = \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})$
‚Ä¢ Volume of Parallelepiped: $V = |[\vec{a} \vec{b} \vec{c}]|$
‚Ä¢ Vector Triple Product (VTP): $\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\vec{c}$

üåü GOLDEN TRICKS FOR JEE

‚Ä¢ Angle: Jab angle nikalna ho toh Dot Product use karein.
‚Ä¢ Coplanar: Agar $[\vec{a} \vec{b} \vec{c}] = 0$, toh vectors ek hi plane mein hain.
‚Ä¢ Lagrange: $|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}|^2 = |\vec{a}|^2|\vec{b}|^2 - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})^2$

üìä MATRICES & DETERMINANTS (FULL CHAPTER SUMMARY) ‚ñº

1. DETERMINANTS: CORE CONCEPTS

Expansion: $3 \times 3$ determinant ko kisi bhi row/column ke along expand kiya ja sakta hai.

Properties (The Game Changers):

  • üîπ Transpose: $|A| = |A^T|$.
  • üîπ Interchange: Agar do rows ya do columns ko interchange karein, toh sign badal jata hai.
  • üîπ Identical: Agar do rows/columns identical (same) hon, toh $|A| = 0$.
  • üîπ Scalar Multiplication: $|kA| = k^n |A|$ (Order $n$ ke liye).
  • üîπ Product Property: $|AB| = |A| |B|$.
  • üîπ Summation: Agar ek row ke elements do terms ka sum hain, toh determinant ko do determinants ke sum mein break kar sakte hain.

2. TYPES OF MATRICES

  • ‚úÖ Symmetric: $A^T = A$.
  • ‚úÖ Skew-Symmetric: $A^T = -A$. (Diagonal elements hamesha 0 hote hain).
  • ‚úÖ Orthogonal: $AA^T = I$ (Iska matlab $|A| = \pm 1$).
  • ‚úÖ Idempotent: $A^2 = A$.
  • ‚úÖ Involutory: $A^2 = I$ (Self-inverse).
  • ‚úÖ Nilpotent: $A^k = 0$ (Kissi power $k$ ke liye matrix null ho jaye).
  • ‚úÖ Unitary: $A^\theta A = I$ (Complex matrices ke liye).

3. ADJOINT AND INVERSE (HIGH WEIGHTAGE)

Adjoint Properties:

  • ‚Ä¢ $A \cdot (adj A) = (adj A) \cdot A = |A| I_n$
  • ‚Ä¢ $|adj A| = |A|^{n-1}$
  • ‚Ä¢ $adj(adj A) = |A|^{n-2} A$
  • ‚Ä¢ $|adj(adj A)| = |A|^{(n-1)^2}$
  • ‚Ä¢ $adj(AB) = (adj B)(adj A)$ (Reverse law)

Inverse ($A^{-1}$): Exist tabhi karega jab $|A| \neq 0$ (Non-Singular).
Relation: $(A^T)^{-1} = (A^{-1})^T$

4. SYSTEM OF LINEAR EQUATIONS (CRAMER'S RULE)

Consistency Conditions:

  • üìå Unique Solution: $D \neq 0$.
  • üìå Infinite Solutions: $D = 0$ AND $D_x = D_y = D_z = 0$.
  • üìå No Solution: $D = 0$ AND kam se kam ek $D_i$ non-zero ho.

5. CAYLEY-HAMILTON THEOREM

Har square matrix apni Characteristic Equation ko satisfy karta hai: $|A - \lambda I| = 0$.

6. ELEMENTARY OPERATIONS

1. $R_i \leftrightarrow R_j$ | 2. $R_i \to k R_i$ | 3. $R_i \to R_i + k R_j$

Note: Determinant ki value sirf teesri operation se change nahi hoti.

üî• GOLDEN TRICKS FOR JEE MAINS

‚Ä¢ Trace (Tr): Sum of principal diagonal elements. $Tr(A+B) = Tr(A) + Tr(B)$.

‚Ä¢ Skew-Symmetric Det: Odd order ($3 \times 3$, $5 \times 5$) ka determinant hamesha 0 hota hai.

‚Ä¢ Differentiation: Row by row differentiate karein aur baki rows constant rakhein.

üåÄ COMPLEX NUMBERS (FULL CHAPTER SUMMARY) ‚ñº

1. BASIC DEFINITION & IOTA ($\iota$)

A number of the form $z = a + i b$, where $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ and $i = \sqrt{-1}$.

  • üîπ $i^2 = -1, i^3 = -i, i^4 = 1$
  • üîπ Sum of four consecutive powers of $i$ is always **zero**: $i^n + i^{n+1} + i^{n+2} + i^{n+3} = 0$.

2. CONJUGATE, MODULUS & ARGUMENT

If $z = x + iy$, then:

  • ‚úÖ Conjugate ($\bar{z}$): $x - iy$ (Reflection in x-axis).
  • ‚úÖ Modulus ($|z|$): $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ (Distance from origin).
  • ‚úÖ Argument ($\theta$): $\tan^{-1}(y/x)$. Principal value lies in $(-\pi, \pi]$.

3. REPRESENTATION FORMS

Euler's Form: $z = r e^{i \theta}$ (Most powerful for multiplication/division).

Polar Form: $z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)$.

De Moivre's Theorem: $(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i \sin(n\theta)$.

4. CUBE ROOTS OF UNITY ($1, \omega, \omega^2$)

  • ‚Ä¢ $1 + \omega + \omega^2 = 0$
  • ‚Ä¢ $\omega^3 = 1$
  • ‚Ä¢ $\omega = \frac{-1 + i\sqrt{3}}{2}$ and $\omega^2 = \frac{-1 - i\sqrt{3}}{2}$

5. GEOMETRY (MOST IMPORTANT)

  • üìç $|z - z_1| = |z - z_2|$ : Perpendicular bisector.
  • üìç $|z - z_1| = r$ : Circle with center $z_1$ and radius $r$.
  • üìç $|z - z_1| + |z - z_2| = 2a$ : Ellipse if $2a > |z_1 - z_2|$.

üî• GOLDEN TRICKS FOR JEE MAINS

‚Ä¢ Rotation Theorem: $\frac{z_3 - z_1}{z_2 - z_1} = \left| \frac{z_3 - z_1}{z_2 - z_1} \right| e^{i \alpha}$.

‚Ä¢ Distance Formula: Distance between $z_1$ and $z_2$ is $|z_1 - z_2|$.

‚Ä¢ Logarithm: $\ln(z) = \ln|z| + i(arg(z) + 2n\pi)$.
